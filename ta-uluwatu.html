Uluwatu Tourist Attractions

Discover the beauty of Bali’s southern cliffs — temples, beaches, and ocean sunsets await in Uluwatu.

Welcome to Uluwatu

Uluwatu is one of Bali’s most breathtaking regions, famous for its towering sea cliffs, surf beaches, and the majestic Uluwatu Temple. It’s a paradise for surfers, photographers, and travelers seeking stunning ocean views.

The area combines spiritual charm, natural beauty, and relaxed beach vibes — making it one of Bali’s top destinations for exploration and relaxation.

1. Pura Luhur Uluwatu

Pura Luhur Uluwatu is one of Bali’s six key sea temples and sits dramatically on a 70-meter-high cliff above the Indian Ocean. It’s famous for its stunning sunsets, cliffside views, and traditional Kecak dance performances.

  • Location: Pecatu, South Kuta, Badung, Bali
  • Opening Hours: 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM
  • Entrance Fee: IDR 50,000 (foreigners)
  • Contact: +62 361 971 001
  • Website: Official Tourism Page

Tip: Come around 5 PM to catch both the Kecak fire dance and the golden sunset view.

2. Padang Padang Beach

Padang Padang Beach is a small yet world-famous beach known for its turquoise water, limestone cliffs, and surf breaks. The beach gained international fame after being featured in the movie Eat Pray Love.

  • Location: Labuan Sait Street, Pecatu, South Kuta, Bali
  • Opening Hours: 7:00 AM – 6:30 PM
  • Entrance Fee: IDR 15,000
  • Facilities: Showers, small cafés, board rentals
  • Contact: +62 812 3673 4222

Tip: Arrive early in the morning to avoid crowds and capture the soft morning light on the cliffs.

3. Suluban Beach (Blue Point)

Suluban Beach, also known as Blue Point, is a hidden gem accessed through rocky caves. It’s a top surf spot with incredible turquoise water and panoramic cliffside views.

  • Location: Jl. Pantai Suluban, Pecatu, South Kuta, Bali
  • Opening Hours: 6:00 AM – 7:00 PM
  • Entrance Fee: Free (parking IDR 5,000)
  • Nearby: Single Fin Bar, Blue Point Bay Villas
  • Contact: +62 812 3819 1400

Tip: Stay after sunset and enjoy drinks at Single Fin Bar — one of Bali’s most scenic cliffside hangouts.
