Uluwatu

Uluwatu

Uluwatu, located on Bali’s southern cliffs, is famous for its breathtaking ocean views and dramatic sunsets. It’s home to one of the island’s most iconic temples perched above the sea — Pura Luhur Uluwatu — and a paradise for surfers.

Beyond its waves, Uluwatu offers a peaceful atmosphere with luxury resorts, scenic beaches, and cultural attractions. The area blends spirituality, adventure, and relaxation in a truly stunning coastal setting.

Tourist Attractions in Uluwatu

Pura Luhur Uluwatu

A sacred sea temple perched on a cliff 70 meters above the Indian Ocean, offering spectacular sunset views and Kecak dance performances.

Top Culinary Spots in Uluwatu

Single Fin

A legendary cliff-top bar and restaurant offering the best ocean views, chill vibes, and signature sunset cocktails.

Address: Jl. Labuan Sait, Uluwatu

El Kabron Cliff Club

Spanish-inspired restaurant and pool club overlooking the Indian Ocean — perfect for fine dining and golden-hour relaxation.

Address: Jl. Pantai Cemongkak, Pecatu

Drifter Café

A cozy surf café serving healthy bowls, artisan coffee, and tropical dishes with a laid-back island atmosphere.

Address: Jl. Labuan Sait, Pecatu

La Baracca

A warm and rustic Italian restaurant loved for its authentic pasta, pizza, and candlelit ambiance.

Address: Jl. Labuan Sait No.107, Pecatu

The Cashew Tree Café

A laid-back café near Bingin Beach, offering healthy meals, smoothie bowls, and a relaxed tropical atmosphere — a favorite among surfers.

Address: Jl. Pantai Bingin No.9, Uluwatu

Warung Bejana

A hidden local gem serving authentic Indonesian food in a relaxed cliffside setting with ocean breezes.

Address: Jl. Raya Uluwatu No.888, Pecatu
