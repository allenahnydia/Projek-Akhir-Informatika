Ubud Tourist Attractions

Explore the cultural and natural heart of Bali through its stunning landscapes and heritage.

Welcome to Ubud

Ubud is the soul of Bali — a serene town surrounded by rice terraces, temples, and art markets. It’s a destination that blends spirituality, creativity, and nature, offering a deeper connection to Balinese culture.

Whether you’re walking through green fields, visiting temples, or dining with a view of the jungle, Ubud gives you an unforgettable experience of peace and inspiration.

1. Tegalalang Rice Terrace

The Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of Bali’s most iconic landscapes. Its layered rice fields offer breathtaking views and a perfect spot for sunrise photography. Visitors can take short walks through the terraces and learn about traditional irrigation systems known as subak.

  • Location: Jalan Raya Tegallalang, Gianyar, Bali
  • Opening Hours: 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • Entrance Fee: IDR 20,000 per person
  • Contact: +62 812 3647 8610
  • Activities: Sightseeing, coffee tasting, swing rides, local art shops

Tip: Come early in the morning for cooler air and softer sunlight — perfect for photos!

2. Ubud Monkey Forest

Officially known as the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary, this nature reserve is home to hundreds of Balinese long-tailed macaques. It’s not just a wildlife attraction — it also houses three ancient temples surrounded by lush rainforest.

  • Location: Jl. Monkey Forest, Ubud, Gianyar
  • Opening Hours: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • Entrance Fee: IDR 80,000 (Adults) / IDR 60,000 (Children)
  • Contact: +62 361 971304
  • Website: www.monkeyforestubud.com

Tip: Avoid bringing loose items — monkeys are playful and may snatch sunglasses or food!

3. Campuhan Ridge Walk

The Campuhan Ridge Walk is a scenic trail offering panoramic views of Ubud’s hills and valleys. It’s a popular spot for morning walks and light hikes surrounded by lush greenery and peaceful atmosphere.

  • Location: Jl. Raya Campuhan, Ubud, Gianyar
  • Opening Hours: 24 hours (best early morning or sunset)
  • Entrance Fee: Free
  • Nearby: Karsa Café, Warwick Ibah Villas, Campuhan Bridge

Tip: Bring water, wear comfortable shoes, and go early to avoid the heat and crowds.
